Core Performance Metrics
Strokes Gained (SG): Putting: This is widely considered the most accurate measure of putting prowess. It compares a golfer's performance from specific distances against a benchmark (like a PGA Tour pro or a scratch golfer), showing exactly how many strokes they gained or lost on the greens.
Three-Putt Frequency: A high-impact stat for amateurs, as three-putting is essentially "throwing away strokes". Golfers track this to identify if they need to work on lag putting and speed control.
Putts Per Green in Regulation (GIR): This is more telling than "Total Putts" because it isolates putting from chipping. If a golfer misses a green and chips it to two feet, a one-putt doesn't necessarily mean they are putting well—it means they chipped well.
